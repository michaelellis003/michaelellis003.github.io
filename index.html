About Me

I am currently a Quantitative Analyst at Black Hills Corporation where I use statistical and machine learning methods to enable efficient learning, enhance understanding and improve decision making. I have been focused on forecasting weather and energy consumption.

I am passionate about using Bayesian methodology to analysis and forecast complex dynamic systems. I particularly love hierarchical Bayesian models, which provide a flexible and intuitive approach to modeling structure in complex systems. I have research experience in Bayesian time series analysis, probabilistic forecasting and stochastic volatility models. In graduate school my research focused on sequential and efficient Bayesian inference of complex state space (time series) models. These methods are commonly used for tracking moving targets, including aircrafts, animals, and even stock price volatility. Check out my master's thesis, Sequential Inference for Hidden Markov Models!

In 2018 I graduated with a master's degree in statistics from the University of Arkansas in Fayetteville and before that I completed my undergraduate degree in applied mathematics. I took courses covering probability theory, statistical inference, time series analysis and forecasting, spatial statistics, computational statistics and machine learning algorithms.
